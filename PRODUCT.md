# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Three distinct audiences:**

1. **Location Partners** – Decision-makers at premium educational institutions (universities, colleges) evaluating vending machine installations. They're looking for modern, tech-enabled solutions that serve their student populations.

2. **Brand Partners** – Marketing and partnerships teams at consumer brands seeking advertising reach and distribution in young, premium demographics. They're evaluating opportunities for ad campaigns (10"/22"/52" display screens) and coil rental (shelf space) in high-traffic student locations.

3. **Potential Investors** – Investment professionals and stakeholders assessing Parrave Ventures as a growth opportunity. They're looking for traction, market positioning, and scalability signals.

## Product Purpose

Parrave Ventures is a new-age smart vending machine operator (not a manufacturer) running 50+ IoT-enabled machines across premium institutions in North India. The website establishes Parrave as a modern, tech-forward startup connecting three value chains: premium campus locations, brand partnerships (advertising + shelf space), and investment opportunity. Success means converting location and brand partnership inquiries, while building investor confidence in the company's growth trajectory.

## Brand Personality

**Approachable, forward-thinking, dynamic**

Young and energetic without being frivolous. Startup confidence without tech-bro arrogance. We're building the infrastructure of modern campus commerce—smart, connected, data-driven—but we're friendly operators, not faceless enterprise. Think: "the vending machine company students actually like" meets "the partnership opportunity brands want to join."

## Anti-references

- **Seaga** (https://seaga.com/) – Manufacturing-focused, industrial, corporate. We're operators, not manufacturers.
- **Vendekin** (https://www.vendekin.com/) – Generic manufacturing aesthetic, enterprise-heavy. Misses the startup energy.
- **Traditional vending company websites** – Dated designs, legacy corporate feel, "saar" business vernacular. We're a tech-enabled startup, not a traditional distributor.
- **Overly polished SaaS** – Don't want the cream-colored, glass-morphism, "enterprise AI" look. We're physical infrastructure with smart tech, not pure software.

The references (Vending Brothers, Daalchini) have useful content structure and messaging, but their visual design is outdated. Use their information architecture, not their aesthetics.

## Design Principles

1. **Operator, not manufacturer** – Every page should reinforce that we run machines, we don't build them. Focus on network reach, IoT capabilities, partnership opportunities, and student engagement—not hardware specs.

2. **Three doors, one house** – Location partners, brand partners, and investors have distinct goals. Make each pathway clear from the homepage, with dedicated flows that speak directly to each audience's decision criteria.

3. **Startup energy, real traction** – Show we're building something fresh and fast-growing (50+ machines, premium institutions, recognizable brand partners), without overplaying or underselling. Concrete numbers and logos do the work; the design carries the energy.

4. **Campus-first context** – Our machines live where students live. The experience should feel native to that young, premium demographic—modern, frictionless, worth talking about—not generic B2B infrastructure.

5. **Data-driven without data-dumping** – IoT and smart infrastructure are core differentiators, but specs and dashboards aren't the hero. Surface insights (foot traffic, engagement, brand reach) as strategic value, not as feature lists.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AA compliance** across all pages
- Ensure proper color contrast for all text (≥4.5:1 for body, ≥3:1 for large text)
- Support reduced motion preferences for animations
- Keyboard navigable throughout
- Screen reader friendly semantic markup

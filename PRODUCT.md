# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Location owners and facility managers** at universities, metro stations, corporate offices, hotels, and residential societies across India. They're evaluating passive income opportunities and convenient amenities for their visitors/occupants. Their context: comparing vending operators, assessing risk vs. reward, seeking hassle-free solutions with zero upfront investment.

## Product Purpose

Parrave Ventures operates smart vending machines across high-traffic Indian locations. The website converts location partners by demonstrating:
1. **Zero-investment model**: Parrave handles all costs, partners earn revenue share
2. **Smart technology**: IoT-enabled machines with cashless payments, real-time analytics
3. **End-to-end operations**: Complete management from installation to maintenance
4. **Proven track record**: 100+ machines, 50+ locations, 99% uptime

Success = partnership inquiries and demo requests from qualified locations.

## Brand Personality

**Professional. Reliable. Modern.**

Parrave positions as a trustworthy B2B partner bringing smart technology to traditional vending. The brand balances:
- **Professional confidence**: Data-driven (statistics, testimonials), transparent revenue model
- **Operational reliability**: 24/7 support, proven uptime, hassle-free promise
- **Modern innovation**: Smart/IoT positioning, cashless payments, tech-forward without being flashy

Voice is direct and benefits-focused. Not salesy or over-promising. Speaks to facility managers making rational business decisions, not consumers seeking excitement.

## Anti-references

**Avoid these patterns:**

1. **Generic SaaS templates**: Blue-and-white corporate blandness, stock business photos, templated hero sections with floating UI mockups. Parrave is a real-world physical service, not cloud software.

2. **Traditional vending industry sites**: Dated industrial aesthetics, machine catalog dumps, engineering-spec focus. Parrave is modern and smart, not legacy mechanical.

3. **Over-designed tech startups**: Excessive gradients everywhere, gratuitous glassmorphism, animated chaos, purple-teal startup palette. Technology serves the business model; don't let design overwhelm the message.

4. **E-commerce product sites**: Direct-to-consumer retail patterns, "Add to Cart" flows, product grids. Parrave is a partnership service, not selling products to consumers.

**Specifically:** The warm cream/sand/beige body background (OKLCH L 0.88-0.95, low chroma, hue 50-80) is the saturated 2026 AI default. Current palette uses clean whites and light grays (#f8f9fa, #f9f9f9), which is appropriate. Don't drift toward cream/parchment/paper tones.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over promises**: Statistics, testimonials, real locations over vague claims. Show the track record.

2. **Partnership clarity**: The zero-investment revenue-sharing model is the core differentiator. Make it immediately clear and transparent.

3. **Smart, not complex**: Emphasize IoT and technology benefits (real-time tracking, cashless, analytics) without technical jargon or overwhelming the user.

4. **Clear next steps**: Every section should move toward consultation/demo request. Remove friction from the conversion path.

5. **Local trust**: India-specific context matters. Metro stations, universities, and cities should feel familiar and credible to Indian location managers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

**WCAG 2.1 AA compliance:**
- 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text
- 3:1 for large text (≥18px or bold ≥14px)
- Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements
- Screen reader support with semantic HTML and ARIA labels
- Reduced motion alternatives for animations (prefers-reduced-motion)
- Alt text for all images
- Form labels and error messages

**Specific considerations:**
- Hero text and CTAs must be high-contrast and readable
- FAQ accordion must be keyboard accessible
- Statistics counters should have static fallback values (not just animated)
- Mobile responsive across devices (many managers will browse on phones)
